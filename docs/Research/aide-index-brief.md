## 1. What the AIDE Index Is

The AIDE (AI-Driven Enterprise Institute) Index is the first comprehensive, objective benchmark of AI maturity across all 500 S&P 500 companies. It was founded by Paul Cheek (MIT Sloan senior lecturer) and launched in full in June 2026.

**Key differentiators for credibility:**
- Uses **only publicly observable data** (OSINT) — no surveys, no self-reporting
- Analysed 518,779 source documents, 271.9M words, 778,384 AI patent families, 10,563 executives profiled
- Four pillars: Literacy, Advocacy, Orientation, Implementation
- Two axes: Strategic Intent (Leadership) vs Operational Integration (Company)
- Cohort-normalised 0–100 scoring across the full S&P 500
- Scores are traceable from composite back to individual data points
- Boston-based independent research company, no pay-to-play

**Source URLs:**
- Main site: https://aideinstitute.com/
- Rankings: https://aideinstitute.com/rankings
- Methodology: https://aideinstitute.com/data-methodology
- Key article — "We Scored Every S&P 500 Company on AI": https://aideinstitute.com/insights/we-scored-every-sp500-company-on-ai
- Key article — "The AI Divide Is Already Here": https://aideinstitute.com/insights/ai-divide-already-here
- Key article — "Why We Don't Use Surveys": https://aideinstitute.com/insights/why-aide-index-doesnt-use-surveys
- CNBC coverage: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/01/nvidia-meta-walmart-among-top-companies-adopting-ai.html

## 4. Stats and Claims to Use — With Caveats

### ✅ USE WITH CONFIDENCE

| Claim | Source | How to cite |
|---|---|---|
| Only 4 of 500 S&P 500 companies scored 100 | AIDE Index, June 2026 | "AIDE Institute, 2026" |
| Stated AI strategy is nearly uncorrelated with actual AI implementation | AIDE "AI Divide" article | "AIDE Institute, 2026" |
| Executives are 3.6x more likely to say "innovation" than "AI" | AIDE "AI Divide" article | "AIDE Institute, 2026" |
| Paul Cheek: "Significant room for improvement" in board/exec AI literacy | CNBC, June 2026 | "Paul Cheek, AIDE Institute CEO, CNBC 2026" |
| Median S&P 500 AIDE score is roughly 42 (computed from sector medians) | AIDE Rankings page | "AIDE Institute, 2026" — but note this is an aggregate estimate |
| Walmart, SLB, AES, NextEra, Ecolab scored in top tier despite being non-tech | AIDE Index | "AIDE Institute, 2026" |
| AIDE uses only publicly observable data, no surveys | AIDE methodology page | Direct link |

### ⚠️ USE WITH QUALIFICATION

| Claim | Issue | How to handle |
|---|---|---|
| "Half of companies scored at or near zero on implementation" | This was from the Brazil pilot, not the S&P 500. The S&P 500 data shows low medians but not literal zeros at the same scale. | Say: "In the AIDE Institute's initial assessment, half of companies scored at or near zero on real AI implementation metrics." Don't say "half of S&P 500 companies scored zero." |
| "Boards don't understand AI" | Cheek said "significant room for improvement" — he didn't say "boards are clueless." | Use his exact framing, not a more inflammatory version. |
| Sector median comparisons | The 42-ish median is an estimate derived from sector medians, not a single published number. AIDE publishes sector medians (55.0 for IT down to 22.19 for Materials) but the overall median isn't a headline they've published. | Either cite the specific sector medians (which are published) or say "the typical S&P 500 company" rather than giving a precise number. |

### ❌ DO NOT CLAIM

| Claim | Why not |
|---|---|
| "AIDE Index proves most AI investment is wasted" | The Index measures adoption posture, not financial returns. They explicitly state this boundary. |
| "Companies that score high on AIDE are more profitable" | No causal link established. The AIDE Institute is actively researching this but hasn't published findings yet. |
| "The AIDE Index endorses AIAI's approach" | No relationship exists. We're citing their public data, not claiming partnership. |
| "Only 4% of S&P 500 companies are doing AI well" | 4/500 is 0.8%, not 4%. And "doing well" is our interpretation, not theirs. Stick to "4 out of 500 scored 100." |
| "AIDE confirms most companies are faking AI" | AIDE doesn't use the word "faking." They say there's a gap between advocacy and implementation. Our tone can be sharper, but don't put words in their data. |

## 8. The AIDE Matrix — Four Archetypes

The AIDE Index classifies companies into four quadrants based on two axes (Strategic Intent vs Operational Integration):

| Archetype | Intent | Integration | AIAI Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trailblazers** | High | High | Rare — only the top companies. We help companies aim here. |
| **Visionaries** | High | Low | "The narrative is ahead of the build." Most common for companies that *think* they're leading. This is where AIAI's work starts — closing the gap. |
| **Stealth Adopters** | Low | High | Quietly building, not talking. Interesting but not our primary market (they're already doing it). |
| **Emerging Adopters / Laggards** | Low | Low | Starting from zero. This is also our market — we help build from the ground up. |

**For our site:** We can frame our services as moving companies from the lower-left toward the upper-right. "Understanding AI" builds Intent. "Proof" demonstrates Integration. Together, they move you toward Trailblazer territory.
